<h1>How to Check AIS, Form 26AS & TIS Before Filing Your [Income Tax](blog.html?id=managed-service-provider-income-tax-portal) Return</h1>

<p>Before submitting your Income Tax Return (ITR), one of the most important steps is verifying your financial information using <strong>Annual Information Statement (AIS)</strong>, <strong>Form 26AS</strong>, and <strong>Taxpayer Information Summary (TIS)</strong>. These three reports help taxpayers reconcile income, taxes deducted, investments, and high-value financial transactions before filing an accurate return.</p>

<p>Many taxpayers simply rely on pre-filled ITR forms. However, differences between your records and information available with the Income Tax Department may result in delayed refunds, notices, additional tax demands or scrutiny. Spending a few minutes reviewing AIS, Form 26AS and TIS can save significant time and money later. The Income Tax Department also encourages taxpayers to review these statements and provide feedback if incorrect information appears. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning">

<strong>Important</strong>

<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">
<li>Always review AIS before filing your ITR.</li>
<li>Verify all TDS entries with Form 26AS.</li>
<li>Cross-check income shown in TIS.</li>
<li>Report any missing or incorrect information before filing.</li>
<li>Keep your own bank statements and investment records handy.</li>
</ul>

</div>

<h2>What are AIS, Form 26AS and TIS?</h2>

<p>Although many taxpayers think these reports are identical, each serves a different purpose. Together, they provide a complete picture of your financial activities during the financial year.</p>

<h2>What Information Appears in AIS?</h2>

<p>The Annual Information Statement is far more comprehensive than the traditional tax statement. It combines information received from employers, banks, mutual funds, registrars, GST systems, reporting entities and other government departments.</p>

<div class="alert alert-info">

<strong>Common Information Available in AIS</strong>

<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">

<li>Salary income</li>

<li>Interest from savings and fixed deposits</li>

<li>Dividend income</li>

<li>Mutual fund purchases and redemptions</li>

<li>Equity share transactions</li>

<li>Property purchase or sale</li>

<li>Foreign remittances</li>

<li>Specified Financial Transactions (SFT)</li>

<li>GST information</li>

<li>Income reported by various financial institutions</li>

<li>TDS and TCS</li>

<li>Advance tax payments</li>

<li>Income tax refunds</li>

</ul>

</div>

<p>AIS has become a comprehensive financial profile of the taxpayer. If information shown is incorrect, duplicate or belongs to another person, taxpayers can submit feedback directly through the AIS portal. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}</p>

<h2>Understanding TIS (Taxpayer Information Summary)</h2>

<p>TIS is a simplified summary generated from AIS. It displays processed values after considering taxpayer feedback wherever applicable. These processed values are generally used for pre-filled ITR data, making return filing easier and more accurate. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}</p>

<h2>How to Download AIS Online</h2>

<p>The Income Tax Department allows every registered taxpayer to view and download the Annual Information Statement (AIS) directly from the e-Filing Portal. You can download AIS in <strong>PDF, JSON, or CSV</strong> format depending on your requirement. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}</p>

<div class="alert alert-primary">

<strong>You'll Need</strong>

<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">

<li>PAN Number</li>

<li>Income Tax e-Filing Portal Login</li>

<li>Password</li>

<li>Registered Mobile Number (if OTP verification is required)</li>

</ul>

</div>

<hr>

<h2>Step-by-Step Guide to View AIS</h2>

<div class="list-group">

<div class="list-group-item">
<strong>Step 1</strong><br>
Visit the Income Tax e-Filing Portal and sign in.
</div>

<div class="list-group-item">
<strong>Step 2</strong><br>
Go to <strong>Services → Annual Information Statement (AIS)</strong>.
</div>

<div class="list-group-item">
<strong>Step 3</strong><br>
Click <strong>Proceed</strong> to open the AIS portal.
</div>

<div class="list-group-item">
<strong>Step 4</strong><br>
Choose the relevant Financial Year.
</div>

<div class="list-group-item">
<strong>Step 5</strong><br>
View AIS or download it in PDF, JSON or CSV format.
</div>

</div>

<p>The official portal also maintains an activity history of your AIS access and allows downloading an acknowledgement after feedback submission. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}</p>
