<h1>15 Common ITR Filing Mistakes That Delay Your Refund or Trigger Tax Notices</h1>

<p>Filing your Income Tax Return (ITR) is much easier today than it was a few years ago, thanks to the Income Tax Department's improved e-filing portal. However, thousands of taxpayers still make small mistakes that can delay refunds, generate notices, increase tax liability, or even require filing an updated return.</p>

<p>Whether you're a salaried employee, pensioner, freelancer, business owner, professional, or first-time taxpayer, understanding these common mistakes can save both time and money.</p>

<p>This guide explains the most frequent ITR filing errors, how to avoid them, and what you should verify before clicking the final <strong>Submit</strong> button.</p>

<h2>Why Small ITR Mistakes Become Expensive</h2>

<p>Many taxpayers assume that once their return is submitted, the process is complete. In reality, every return is compared against multiple databases including:</p>

<ul>

<li>Employer TDS returns</li>

<li>Banks and financial institutions</li>

<li>Form 26AS</li>

<li>Annual Information Statement (AIS)</li>

<li>Taxpayer Information Summary (TIS)</li>

<li>Mutual fund transactions</li>

<li>Stock market transactions</li>

<li>Property registrations</li>

<li>GST records (for businesses)</li>

</ul>

<p>Even a minor mismatch can result in refund delays, additional tax demands, or notices from the Income Tax Department.</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning">

<strong>Tip:</strong>

Always compare your return with your AIS, TIS, Form 26AS and salary documents before submission.

</div>

<p>This is one of the biggest mistakes made by taxpayers every year.</p>

<p>Different categories of taxpayers are required to file different ITR forms. Filing the wrong form may result in the return being treated as defective.</p>

<h2>Mistake #2 — Not Checking Form 26AS</h2>

<p>Many taxpayers directly enter salary or tax deducted from Form 16 without checking Form 26AS.</p>

<p>Form 26AS reflects taxes deposited with the government. If the TDS shown in your return doesn't match Form 26AS, refund processing may get delayed.</p>

<div class="alert alert-success">

Before filing your ITR, compare:

<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">

<li>Employer TDS</li>

<li>Bank TDS</li>

<li>Advance tax</li>

<li>Self-assessment tax</li>

<li>TCS credits</li>

</ul>

</div>

<h2>Mistake #5 — Missing Dividend Income</h2>

<p>Companies and mutual funds often credit dividends directly into bank accounts.</p>

<p>Taxpayers sometimes forget to include this income while filing returns because no separate payment reminder is received.</p>

<p>However, dividend transactions generally appear in AIS and may also be reflected through other reporting mechanisms.</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning">

Always verify dividend entries before submitting your return.

<h2>Mistake #10 — Filing Without Verifying the Return</h2>

<p>Many taxpayers believe filing is complete once the return is submitted.</p>

<p>However, the return generally needs to be verified through the prescribed verification methods unless processed automatically under applicable procedures.</p>

<div class="alert alert-danger">

<strong>Important:</strong>

An unverified return may be treated as not validly filed if verification is not completed within the prescribed time.

</div>

<hr>

<h2>Mistake #11 — Waiting Until the Last Day</h2>

<p>One of the biggest mistakes every year is waiting until the filing deadline.</p>

<p>Although the Income Tax portal has become much more stable, last-minute filing increases the chances of:</p>

<ul>

<li>Internet connectivity issues</li>

<li>Heavy portal traffic</li>

<li>Payment failures</li>

<li>Banking delays</li>

<li>Document mistakes</li>

<li>Missing important information</li>

</ul>

<div class="alert alert-info">

Early filing also allows you to receive your refund sooner if all information is correct.

</div>

<hr>

<h2>Mistake #12 — Ignoring Income From Previous Employers</h2>

<p>If you changed jobs during the financial year, you may have received salary from more than one employer.</p>

<p>Many taxpayers accidentally report income from only their latest employer.</p>

<p>This may result in under-reporting of salary income and additional tax demand later.</p>

<h2>Mistake #15 — Filing Without Professional Review</h2>

<p>Simple ITRs may be straightforward, but returns involving business income, capital gains, multiple employers, foreign assets, or complex deductions often benefit from an independent review before submission.</p>

<p>A second review can help identify missing income, incorrect deductions, calculation errors, and reporting inconsistencies.</p>
