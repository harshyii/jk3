<h2>What is an On Grid Solar System?</h2>

<p>

An On Grid Solar System (also known as a Grid-Tied Solar System) is connected directly to the electricity distribution network. During the day, electricity generated by solar panels powers your building first. Any surplus electricity is exported to the utility grid through a net meter.

</p>

<p>

At night or during cloudy weather, electricity is automatically imported from the grid. Since batteries are not required, on-grid systems are generally more affordable than hybrid or off-grid solar systems.

</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning">

<strong>Main Components of an On Grid Solar System</strong>

<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">

<li>Solar PV Panels</li>

<li>Grid-Tie Solar Inverter</li>

<li>Module Mounting Structure</li>

<li>DC & AC Cables</li>

<li>Safety Protection Devices</li>

<li>Earthing System</li>

<li>Lightning Protection</li>

<li>Net Meter</li>

</ul>
