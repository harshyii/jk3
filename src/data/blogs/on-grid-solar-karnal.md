<h2>What is an On-Grid Solar System?</h2>

<p>

An on-grid solar system (also called a <strong>grid-tied solar system</strong>) is directly connected to the electricity distribution network. During daytime, solar panels generate electricity that powers your building first. Any excess energy is exported back to the electricity grid through net metering, while additional power is automatically imported whenever solar generation is insufficient.

</p>

<div class="text-center my-4">

<h5>Solar Panels → Inverter → Building Load → Electricity Grid</h5>

</div>

<p>

Unlike off-grid systems, on-grid solar installations do not require large battery banks, making them more affordable and easier to maintain.

</p>

<h2>How Net Metering Works</h2>

<p>

One of the biggest advantages of an on-grid solar system is <strong>net metering</strong>. During sunny hours, your solar panels may generate more electricity than your building consumes. Instead of wasting that electricity, the excess power is exported to the utility grid. At night or during cloudy weather, electricity is imported from the grid as required.

</p>

<div class="alert alert-primary">

<strong>Simple Flow</strong>

<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">

<li>Solar powers your building first.</li>

<li>Extra electricity is exported to the grid.</li>

<li>The net meter records exported and imported units.</li>

<li>Your electricity bill is adjusted according to applicable regulations.</li>

</ul>

</div>
