<h2>What Is an On Grid Solar System?</h2>

<p>

An <strong>On Grid Solar System</strong>, also known as a <strong>Grid-Tied Solar System</strong>, generates electricity using solar panels while remaining connected to the government electricity grid. During the day, your solar panels power your home or business first. If additional electricity is required, it is automatically supplied by the grid.

</p>

<p>

When your solar panels generate more electricity than you consume, the excess power is exported through a <strong>Net Meter</strong>, allowing credits to accumulate according to local electricity regulations.

</p>

<h2>Understanding Net Metering</h2>

<p>

Net metering allows your electricity meter to record both the electricity consumed from the grid and the excess electricity exported by your solar system. During sunny hours, any surplus power generated by your panels is supplied back to the grid. When solar production is low, such as at night, electricity is drawn from the grid as usual.

</p>

<div class="alert alert-primary">

<strong>Simple Example</strong>

<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">

<li>Your home consumes 20 units.</li>

<li>Your solar plant generates 28 units.</li>

<li>20 units are used immediately.</li>

<li>8 surplus units are exported through the net meter.</li>

</ul>

</div>
